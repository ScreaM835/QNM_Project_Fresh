ABSTRACT
--------
PINNs compute the first QNMs of Kerr via the Teukolsky equation. Almost-
immediate PyTorch implementation; no sophisticated numerical infrastructure.
Computes ω and the separation constant A for arbitrary BH spin and mass with
typical errors <1 % w.r.t. literature. PINN-derived QNMs are
indistinguishable from accepted values for SNR > ~100 ⇒ reliable for GW data
analysis with current-generation detectors, before LISA / Einstein
Telescope where SNR ~O(1000) is expected.


I. INTRODUCTION
==============================================================================
* Motivation: GW BBH detections (LIGO-Virgo-KAGRA) opened black-hole
  spectroscopy. QNMs = "fingerprint" of relaxing BH after perturbation
  (outgoing at infinity, ingoing at horizon).
* No-hair test requires precise ID of ≥3 QNM parameters; SNR ~O(100)
  needed. Currently a stretch for 2nd-gen detectors but feasible for ET
  (Einstein Telescope) and LISA (3rd-gen).
* QNM master equations: Schwarzschild perturbations (Regge-Wheeler 1957;
  Zerilli 1970; Vishveshwara 1970) extended to Kerr by Teukolsky (1973).
* Existing methods: Leaver continued fraction (1985), time evolution,
  bound-state effective potentials, WKB (Iyer-Will 1987), AIM (Cho 2012),
  pseudospectral (Cook-Zalutskiy 2014; Jansen 2017), large-D expansion.
* This paper: PINN approach; previous Schwarzschild-only PINN work
  (Övgün 2021, Ncube 2021, Cornell 2022) extended to Kerr.
* PINN advantages:
  - Mesh-free; AD computes derivatives at arbitrary points.
  - Multiple coupled equations & multiple eigenvalues handled by
    weighted-sum loss.
  - PDE coefficients need not be analytic ⇒ works on numerical
    backgrounds.
  - Implementation = a few lines of PyTorch.


II. THE TEUKOLSKY EQUATION
==============================================================================
Tensor (s = −2) perturbations of Kerr; separation of variables (eq 1):
  ψ = (1/2π) ∫ e^{-iωt} Σ_{ℓ=|s|}^∞ Σ_{m=-ℓ}^ℓ e^{imφ} S_ℓm(u) R_ℓm(r) dω.
Geometrised units G = c = 2M = 1 (M reinstated for waveforms).
RADIAL EQ (eq 2):
  Δ(r) R″(r) + (s+1)(2r − 1) R′(r) + V(r) R(r) = 0,
with
  Δ(r)  = r² − r + a²,
  V(r)  = α(r) ω² + β(r) ω + γ(r),
  α(r)  = (r² + a²)²/Δ(r) − a²,
  β(r)  = i s (a² − r²) − 2amr / Δ(r) + 2 i s r,
  γ(r)  = a² m² + i s am (2r/Δ − 1) − A.
Schwarzschild limit (eq 4):  lim_{a→0} A = ℓ(ℓ+1) − s(s+1).
ANGULAR EQ (eq 5):
  (1 − u²) S″(u) − 2u S′(u)
    + [a²ω² u² − 2aωs u + s + A − (m + s u)²/(1 − u²)] S(u) = 0,
where u = cos θ.
Boundary conditions: ingoing at horizon, outgoing at infinity, regular at
poles θ = 0, π.
LEAVER ANSÄTZE (eq 6, 7) — absorb BCs:
  R(r) = e^{iωr} (r − r_-)^{p_-} (r − r_+)^{p_+} f(x),
    p_- = -1 - s + iω + iσ_+,
    p_+ = -s − iσ_+,
    σ_+ = (ω r_+ − a m)/√(1 − 4a²),
    x   = r_+/r ∈ [0, 1] (compactified radial domain).
  S(u) = e^{aωu} (1 + u)^{|m−s|/2} (1 − u)^{|m+s|/2} g(u).
With r_± = (1 ± √(1 − 4a²))/2.
After substitution, residual equations (eq 8):
  L_F[f(x)] = F_2 f″(x) + F_1 f′(x) + F_0 f(x) = 0
  L_G[g(u)] = G_2 g″(u) + G_1 g′(u) + G_0 g(u) = 0.
Coefficients in Appendix A (lengthy polynomials in x, u, ω, A, m, r_+, a).
Normalisations (eq 9):  f(1) = 1,  g(−1) = 1.


III. THE NEURAL NETWORK
==============================================================================
* Two FNNs N_f, N_g approximate f(x) and g(u) independently.
* Each: 1 input neuron, 2 output neurons (Re, Im); 2 hidden layers × 200
  neurons; tanh activation. Real-arithmetic everywhere.
* Trainable parameters: weights/biases AND eigenvalues ω, A.
* Uniform grid (eq 10):
    x_i = (i − 1)/(N_x − 1), i = 1,…,N_x = 100;
    u_j = 2(j − 1)/(N_u − 1) − 1, j = 1,…,N_u = 100.
* LOSS (eq 11):
    L = (W/N_x) Σ_i |L_F[f(x_i)]| + (1/N_u) Σ_j |L_G[g(u_j)]|,
  with W = 10 (radial weighted higher).
* HARD ENFORCEMENT of (eq 9), eq 12:
    f(x) = (e^{x-1} − 1) N_f(x) + 1
    g(u) = (e^{u+1} − 1) N_g(u) + 1.
* Optimisation: Adam (Kingma-Ba), lr_0 = 0.005, exp decay 0.999/epoch,
  2000 epochs/spin value.
* Initial weights: normal (μ=0, σ=0.05 for f; σ=0.01 for g); biases zero.
* Initial seeds: ω_0 = 0.7 − 0.1 i; A_0 = ℓ(ℓ+1) − s(s+1).
* SPIN SCAN (eq 13):
    a_k = ½ (1 − 10^{-3(k-1)/(N_a-1)}),  k = 1,…,N_a,  N_a = 20.
  Clusters near extremality. Warm-start each spin from previous spin's
  trained weights ⇒ ensures convergence to same ℓ-mode.

III.1 Hyperparameter study
---------------------------
Figs 2-3: error vs # neurons/layer and # hidden layers, for (ℓ,m,n)=
(2,0,0), spinless and a=0.4999. Once "sufficient complexity", accuracy
plateau. CHOICE: 2 × 200 tanh; 2000 iter ≈ 2 min on Intel i7-10750H @
2.60 GHz CPU. Errors < 1 %.

IV.2 Detectability Prospects
-----------------------------
Ringdown strain model (eq 14):
  h = h_0 e^{-t/τ} cos(2π f t + φ).
Mass-rescaled (eq 15):
  f = c³ Re[ω] / (4π G M),     τ = -2 G M / (c³ Im[ω]).
Compare PINN waveforms vs the Python pykerr library (which uses tabulated
Berti values, GRIT website).
MATCH (eq 17):
  F(a, b) = max_{t_c, φ} (a|b) / √[(a|a)(b|b)],
INNER PRODUCT (eq 18, weighted by aLIGOZeroDetHighPower PSD):
  (a|b) = 4 Re ∫_{f_0}^{f_max} ã(f) b̃*(f) / S_n(f) df.
DISTINGUISHABILITY (eq 16):
  F = 1 − D²/(2 SNR_min²),   D = 4 (mass, spin, amplitude, phase).
RESULT: SNR_min > 100 for spins ≲ 0.9 — typical of LIGO-Virgo events ⇒
PINN QNMs are usable for parameter estimation in the near term, before
3rd-gen detectors. Quality decays as a → extremality, especially for
(2,2,0).


V. DISCUSSION
==============================================================================
* Demonstrated PINN solution of full Teukolsky for the first time.
  Targets: (2,2,0), (2,0,0); subdominants (3,3,0), (2,2,1) shown.
* Validates PINN as a viable tool for BH spectroscopy in current-detector
  regime (SNR ≲ 100).
* Strengths: trivial generalisation, very few lines of code, multiple
  eigenvalues at once via weighted loss sum, no analytic coefficients
  required ⇒ promising for numerical backgrounds (exotic compact
  objects, modified gravity).
* Weaknesses: slower & less accurate than spectral methods. Not a
  concern here because (i) tiny architecture trains in ~2 min/mode,
  (ii) precision is enough for current GW SNR.
* Outlook: extend to broader strong-field-gravity scenarios with
  numerically given backgrounds.
