\section{Discussion}\label{sec:discussion}

\paragraph{Relation to prior work.}
The reproduction of Sec.~\ref{sec:res-repro} makes the
comparison with \citet{Patel2024pinn} exact; the further
improvement to $\mathrm{RL2}=0.58\%$
(Table~\ref{tab:fwd-summary}) is accounted for by the doubled
L-BFGS budget and the greedy adaptive resampling of
Sec.~\ref{sec:fwd-pinn}. The sub-permille eigenvalue PINN of
\citet{Cornell2022pinnqnm} is frequency-domain and not directly
comparable to a time-domain post-hoc fit; the hard boundary
enforcement of \citet{Luna2022teukolskypinn} is a natural
variant of our soft setup.

\paragraph{The plateau as a quality flag.}
The plateau standard deviation $\sigma_\omega$ estimates the
fit-window systematic and simultaneously acts as a validity
flag: a small $\sigma_\omega$
certifies the estimate, a large or undefined one rejects it. M5
generalises the scan to the joint $(t_0,\tend)$ plane and
becomes necessary when the window edge approaches
$t_{\mathrm{tail}}$: when too little ringdown remains inside the
window, no block of fits is stationary and the flag correctly
withholds the estimate.

\paragraph{Detector-unit translation.}
For a GW150914-class Schwarzschild remnant
($M = 65\,M_\odot$) the Leaver values used throughout
correspond to $f_{220} \approx 186\,\mathrm{Hz}$ and
$\tau_0 \approx 3.60\,\mathrm{ms}$, and the enhanced forward
PINN's fractional errors map to
$\sigma_{f_{220}} \approx 0.05\,\mathrm{Hz}$ and
$\sigma_{\tau_0} \approx 72\,\mu\mathrm{s}$ --- at least an
order of magnitude below the statistical uncertainties reported
at ringdown signal-to-noise $\rho_{\mathrm{ring}} \sim 8$--$10$
\citep{LIGO2016ringdown,Isi2019tests}. At current sensitivities
the limiting uncertainty is therefore the data noise rather
than the network systematics; the balance inverts at the
per-mille sensitivities forecast for next-generation detectors
\citep{Berti2007ringdown}.

\paragraph{Limitations.}
Of the two original scope choices, the fixed Schwarzschild
background is lifted in Sec.~\ref{sec:res-kerr}, where the
hybrid is retrained on rotating (Kerr) backgrounds via the
Teukolsky operator; the single quadrupole channel
$\Phi(\xq,t)$ lifts analogously to one head per harmonic. The
substantive limitation is the ill-conditioning of ESPRIT
on a nearly clean damped sinusoid, which excludes M3 from the
primary numbers.

A related scope point concerns the overtone. The two-mode M4
template is credited for the sub-percent fundamental-mode
accuracy (the overtone term absorbs $n=1$ contamination;
\citealt{Giesler2019overtones}), but reporting
$(M\omega_1,\tau_1/M)$ as a separate result is not warranted:
our Gaussian initial data excite $n=1$ far more weakly than the
numerical-relativity remnants on which the Giesler prescription
was demonstrated, and the plateau-mean overtone recovers
$M\omega_1$ no better than $\sim\!20\%$. Overtone-exciting
initial data are a natural follow-up, given the active
controversy over the GW150914 overtone
\citep{Cotesta2022overtones,Isi2021priors}.

\paragraph{Amortised surrogates.}
Each PINN must be trained anew for every black hole; the hybrid
trains once and then serves any in-distribution draw at the
cost of one coarse solve plus one forward pass. Its gain is structural: the coarse-prior
error correlates strongly with the prior gradient, so the
parameter-free gate routes network capacity onto the wavefronts
where the prior fails, cutting the population field MSE by
$\sim\!46\times$ while protecting the regions the classical
solve already resolves (Sec.~\ref{sec:res-hyb}); the field gain
does not become a median QNM gain because the
coarse-up baseline already sits at the extractor floor at the
common observer.

\paragraph{Geometry-agnostic generalisation.}
The Kerr experiment of Sec.~\ref{sec:res-kerr} shows that the
hybrid is not tied to the Schwarzschild wave equation. The same
coarse-plus-residual operator, retrained on the complex,
hyperboloidal Teukolsky field across spins $a/M \in [0,0.95]$,
cuts the median field error by $77\times$ and recovers the
near-extremal fundamental frequency by an order of magnitude
($4.71\%\to0.38\%$), precisely where the coarse prior fails
fastest. This is the qualitative advantage of an amortised
surrogate over a per-instance PINN: a PINN must be retrained
from scratch for every spin, whereas the operator transfers
across the parameter family at one coarse solve plus one
forward pass. The damping time remains the harder channel on
Kerr just as on Schwarzschild --- the residual correction,
tuned to the field and its leading oscillation, does not
sharpen the exponential envelope, so $\tau$ stays a fine-solve
quantity. This is an intrinsic property of correcting a field
rather than a decay rate, not a Kerr-specific artefact.

\paragraph{Methodological outlook.}
Recasting the point estimate as a Bayesian posterior over
$(\omega,\tau)$ with the trained PINN as the forward operator
\citep{Isi2021priors} would combine the network-systematic
uncertainty (which the plateau diagnostic estimates) with the
data-statistical uncertainty that a point estimate lacks; a
Cram\'er--Rao baseline completes it. On the training side, the
static loss weights could be replaced by adaptive
\citep{Wang2021gradientpath,Wang2022ntk,McClenny2023sapinn} or
causal time-weighting \citep{Wang2022causal} schemes ---
natural for ringdown, where the early-time pulse must be
resolved before the late-time decay can be trusted --- and the
soft Sommerfeld boundaries enforced hard via an output
transform \citep{Luna2022teukolskypinn}.
