\section{Supplementary scans and diagnostics}
\label{app:supp}

This appendix presents the parameter scans used to select the
gradient-gate scale and QNM fit windows.

\subsection{Schwarzschild hFNO gradient-gate diagnostics}
\label{app:supp-gate}

Figure~\ref{fig:hyb-grad} relates numerical-prior error to
$d_{\mathrm{idx}}$. Table~\ref{tab:hyb-gate-decile} reports the error
reduction in the four highest deciles; lower deciles have substantially
smaller numerical-prior errors.

\begin{figure}[!htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.82\textwidth]{../outputs/hybrid/fno_sw_gate_s1em3_t100/figs/grad_vs_error.png}
\caption{Spatial correspondence between numerical-prior error (left),
index-space gradient magnitude $d_{\mathrm{idx}}$ (centre), and hFNO
error (right) on the canonical Schwarzschild configuration. All
panels use the same logarithmic colour scale. Across all cells, the
correlation between numerical-prior error and $d_{\mathrm{idx}}$ is
$r=0.921$.}
\label{fig:hyb-grad}
\end{figure}

\subsection{Plateau-scan diagnostics}
\label{app:supp-plateau}

The M4 and M5 plateau constructions of Sec.~\ref{sec:plateau}
underpin the frequency and damping-time entries of
Tables~\ref{tab:fwd-qnm} and~\ref{tab:hyb-canonical}.
Figures~\ref{fig:fwd-plateau-m4}--\ref{fig:hyb-plateau-m5}
show the scans for the forward PINN and the Schwarzschild hFNO.

\begin{figure}[!htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.95\textwidth]{../outputs/qnm/zerilli_l2_greedy_f03_lbfgs30k/pinn_method4_stability.png}
\caption{M4 plateau diagnostic for the forward PINN.
$(\omega_0,\tau_0)$ as a function of the start time $t_0$ on
$[10,25]\,M$ at fixed $\tend = 50\,M$, with the eight-window
plateau block ($t_0\in[14,21]\,M$) shaded.}
\label{fig:fwd-plateau-m4}
\end{figure}

\begin{figure}[!htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.85\textwidth,keepaspectratio]{../outputs/qnm/zerilli_l2_greedy_f03_lbfgs30k/pinn_method5_2d_heatmap.png}
\caption{M5 2-D plateau scan for the forward PINN. Heatmaps of
the fitted $\omega$ (left) and $\tau$ (right) over the
$(t_0,\tend)$ grid, on the shared colour scales
$\omega \in [0.30, 0.50]$ and $\tau \in [5, 20]\,M$. The red
rectangle marks the identified plateau block
($t_0 \in [10.0, 16.7]\,M$, $\tend \in [42.0, 50.0]\,M$). White

\end{figure}
