\section{Supplementary scans and diagnostics}
\label{app:supp}

This appendix collects the parameter scans, ablations and
diagnostics that justify the configuration choices reported in
Sec.~\ref{sec:results}.

\subsection{Inverse PINN: template diagnostics}
\label{app:supp-inv}

Tables~\ref{tab:inv-learned} and~\ref{tab:tau-degeneracy} list
the learned template scalars for the six inverse variants and
compare $\tau_{\mathrm{learned}}$ with the post-hoc M2
wide-window fit: the strongly template-weighted variants (A, D,
E) drift $1.2$--$1.9\,M$ below the post-hoc value along the
$A_{\mathrm{ring}}$--$\tau$ degeneracy of
Eq.~\eqref{eq:loss-ring} while the field retains the physical
decay rate --- the quantitative basis for the reporting
convention of Sec.~\ref{sec:reporting}.

\subsection{FNO: observer-position sweep}
\label{app:supp-xq}

Figure~\ref{fig:fno-xq-sweep} reports all four estimators at
observer positions $\xq \in \{0,2,5,10\}\,M$ on the canonical
BH, for the FNO and the FD reference alike. The plateau
estimator validates at $\xq = 2\,M$ on both fields; at
$\xq = 10\,M$ the ringdown amplitude falls below the FD
reference's own error floor within the $[10,100]\,M$ window and
neither field yields a plateau --- the failure is a property of
the window--observer geometry, not of the surrogate.

\begin{figure}[!htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{../outputs/qnm/fno_xq2_comparison/figs_v4/fno_v4_xq_sweep.png}
\caption{QNM extraction across observer positions
$\xq \in \{0,2,5,10\}\,M$ on the canonical BH for the FNO
(blue) and the FD reference (red), for all four estimators.
At $\xq = 0$ and $\xq = 5$, M1 (FFT) is the strongest estimator;
at $\xq = 2$, M1/M2 saturate at $\sim 100\%$ (the field
sign-changes inside the prompt pulse support) and M4 dominates
at permille-level precision; at $\xq = 10$, M4 returns no plateau
on either the FNO field or the FD reference field within the
$t \in [10,100]\,M$ window, indicating that the M4 failure mode
is a property of the window--observer geometry rather than of
the operator surrogate.}
\label{fig:fno-xq-sweep}
\end{figure}

\subsection{FNO: design ablation, homogeneity, resolution transfer and cost}
\label{app:supp-fno}

Table~\ref{tab:fno-versions} reports the three-configuration
design ablation. Configuration I ($t_{\max} = 50\,M$) attains
the best canonical field RMSD but cannot resolve the damping
time ($344\%$ canonical $\tau$ error): fifty $M$ is fewer than
five fundamental damping times. Configuration II doubles the
window and the temporal modes, recovering the population-median
QNM to sub-percent level; the deployed configuration III adds
the observer-slice and time-weighted loss terms, recovering the
canonical-BH extraction by two orders of magnitude at a
marginal ($\sim\!25\%$) increase in canonical field RMSD.

\subsection{Hybrid: gradient-gate diagnostics}
\label{app:supp-gate}

Figure~\ref{fig:hyb-grad} shows the per-cell scatter of
coarse-prior error against prior gradient magnitude on the
canonical BH (log--log Pearson $r = 0.917$), the empirical
basis of the gradient gate, and
Table~\ref{tab:hyb-gate-decile} the decile stratification: the
gated correction reduces the mean error by $12.3\times$ in the
top gradient decile, declining monotonically to $2.6\times$ in
the sixth, with the sub-median cells left at the prior floor.
The gate scale itself is fixed label-free in
Appendix~\ref{app:gate-scale}.

\begin{figure}[!htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.82\textwidth]{../outputs/hybrid/fno_sw_gate_s1em3/figs/grad_vs_error.png}
\caption{Coarse-prior error versus prior gradient on the
canonical BH, the diagnostic motivating the gradient gate.
Each point is one $(x,t)$ cell; the horizontal axis is the
prior gradient magnitude $|\nabla\mathcal{U}\Phi^{\mathrm{c}}|$
and the vertical axis the coarse-up baseline error
$|\Phi^{\mathrm{f}} - \mathcal{U}\Phi^{\mathrm{c}}|$, both on
log scales. The log--log Pearson correlation is $r = 0.917$:
the prior error is large precisely on the high-gradient
wavefronts and small in the low-gradient interior and causal
exterior, so the gate $g$ (Eq.~\eqref{eq:hyb-gate}) routes the
network correction to the cells that need it.}
\label{fig:hyb-grad}
\end{figure}
