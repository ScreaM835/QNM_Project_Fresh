\section{Supplementary scans and diagnostics}
\label{app:supp}

This appendix collects the parameter scans and
diagnostics that justify the configuration choices reported in
Sec.~\ref{sec:results}.

\subsection{Hybrid: gradient-gate diagnostics}
\label{app:supp-gate}

Figure~\ref{fig:hyb-grad} (log--log Pearson $r = 0.921$) and
Table~\ref{tab:hyb-gate-decile} are the empirical basis of the
gate: the correction cuts the mean error by $14.1\times$ in the
top gradient decile, falling to $2.6\times$ by the sixth, with
the sub-median cells left at the prior floor. The scale is fixed
label-free in Appendix~\ref{app:gate-scale}.

\begin{figure}[!htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.82\textwidth]{../outputs/hybrid/fno_sw_gate_s1em3_t100/figs/grad_vs_error.png}
\caption{Coarse-prior error versus prior gradient on the
canonical BH, each point one $(x,t)$ cell with both axes on log
scales. The strong correlation ($r = 0.921$) confirms that the
prior error concentrates on the high-gradient wavefronts, which
the gate $g$ (Eq.~\eqref{eq:hyb-gate}) targets.}
\label{fig:hyb-grad}
\end{figure}

\begin{table}[!htbp]
\centering
\caption{Hybrid error reduction stratified by coarse-prior

\subsection{Plateau-scan diagnostics}
\label{app:supp-plateau}

The M4 and M5 plateau constructions of Sec.~\ref{sec:plateau}
underpin the frequency and damping-time entries of
Tables~\ref{tab:fwd-qnm} and~\ref{tab:hyb-canonical}.
Figures~\ref{fig:fwd-plateau-m4}--\ref{fig:hyb-plateau-m5}
collect the scans for the forward PINN and the hybrid surrogate.
The canonical hybrid M5 scan recovers the frequency to
$0.047\%$, consistent with the hybrid M4 plateau.

\begin{figure}[!htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.95\textwidth]{../outputs/qnm/zerilli_l2_greedy_f03_lbfgs30k/pinn_method4_stability.png}
\caption{M4 plateau diagnostic for the forward PINN.
$(\omega_0,\tau_0)$ as a function of the start time $t_0$ on
$[10,25]\,M$ at fixed $\tend = 50\,M$, with the eight-window
plateau block ($t_0\in[14,21]\,M$) shaded.}
\label{fig:fwd-plateau-m4}
\end{figure}
