\section{Results}\label{sec:results}

We report four numerical tests. The first two revisit
the Schwarzschild finite-radius problem, first as a reproduction of
\citet{Patel2024pinn} and then with the enhanced
forward PINN. The third test evaluates the Schwarzschild hFNO on the
canonical configuration and an independent set of $100$
Schwarzschild configurations. The fourth test evaluates the Kerr hFNO
on an independent set of $128$ Kerr configurations,
followed by extraction of the spin-dependent $(4,2,0)$ mode at
$\scri$.

Table~\ref{tab:results-summary} summarises the main field and QNM
errors. Field errors are measured against the FD reference for the
Schwarzschild experiments and against the fine hyperboloidal
reference field for Kerr. QNM errors are reported under the convention of
Sec.~\ref{sec:reporting}. The Schwarzschild QNM errors use
$M\omega_0^R = 0.3737$ and $\tau_0/M = 11.241$ at $\xq = 10\,M$.
Kerr errors use the spin-dependent Leaver value of the $(4,2,0)$ mode
at $\scri$. Per-estimator results and plateau scans appear below and
in Appendix~\ref{app:supp}.

\paragraph{Field accuracy.} At matched settings, the Zerilli and
Regge--Wheeler PINNs reach \textbf{$2.61\%$ and $2.67\%$ relative
$L^2$ error} (RMSD $3.68\times10^{-3}$ and
$3.77\times10^{-3}$), compared with $28.06\%$ and $23.59\%$ in
\citet[Table~2]{Patel2024pinn}. These factors of approximately $11$
and $9$ were obtained using double-precision training and the outgoing
initial velocity described in Sec.~\ref{sec:fwd-pinn}; their
individual contributions were not isolated. The remaining error is
concentrated on the outgoing wavefront.

\paragraph{QNM accuracy.} Table~\ref{tab:repro-qnm} compares M1 and
M2 with \citet[Table~3]{Patel2024pinn}. Frequencies agree at the
one-to-two-percent level, while the M2 damping-time error improves
from $14.42\%$ to $1.27\%$ for Zerilli and from $5.39\%$ to
$1.56\%$ for Regge--Wheeler.

\paragraph{Isospectral consistency.} The M2 damping-time estimates
for the two parity sectors are consistent with their shared QNM spectrum
\citep{Chandrasekhar1975}. We therefore use the Zerilli equation for
the remaining Schwarzschild experiments.

\subsection{Enhanced forward PINN}
\label{sec:res-fwd}

the L-BFGS budget and replaces uniform resampling with
residual-greedy sampling (Sec.~\ref{sec:fwd-pinn}).

\paragraph{Field accuracy.} The enhanced PINN reaches
\textbf{$0.58\%$ relative $L^2$ error} and RMSD
$8.17\times10^{-4}$ against the FD reference. This is a factor of
approximately $50$ smaller than the $28.06\%$ reported by
\citet[Table~2]{Patel2024pinn} and improves on the $2.61\%$
reproduction. The remaining error follows the wavefronts in
Figs.~\ref{fig:fwd-field} and~\ref{fig:fwd-heatmap}.

\paragraph{QNM accuracy.} Table~\ref{tab:fwd-qnm} reports M1--M5;
Appendix~\ref{app:supp-plateau} gives the plateau diagnostics.

M4 gives the reported frequency error, $0.029\%$, whereas M2 gives

spread is well below the $2.65\%$ bias, which is consistent with
residual error in the PINN waveform or inadequacy of the two-mode
template.

\paragraph{Field accuracy.} On the canonical configuration the
Schwarzschild hFNO reaches \textbf{$1.24\%$ relative $L^2$ error}
and RMSD $1.70\times10^{-3}$, compared with $11.20\%$ and
$1.53\times10^{-2}$ for the numerical prior. Across $100$
configurations, the mean relative $L^2$ error is \textbf{$1.85\%$},
compared with $13.39\%$ for the prior, and the field MSE falls by a
factor of $46$.
The remaining error follows the wavefronts and late-time ringdown.

\paragraph{QNM accuracy.} Across $100$ configurations, the median M4
frequency and damping-time errors are $0.152\%$ and $0.528\%$,
compared with fine-reference extraction errors of $0.064\%$ and
$0.092\%$. The excess
damping-time error is consistent with the late-time residual in
Fig.~\ref{fig:hyb-ringdown-xq2}. On the canonical configuration, M5
gives \textbf{$0.047\%$ frequency error} and M4 gives
\textbf{$1.31\%$ damping-time error} using the $t\simeq80\,M$
usable-signal cutoff.

\paragraph{Field accuracy.} Across the $128$ configurations, the
median relative $L^{2}$ error decreases from $59.5\%$ for the
numerical prior to $0.78\%$ for the Kerr hFNO. This is a factor-of-$77$
reduction. The hFNO bin median rises modestly from $0.70\%$ at low spin
to $1.08\%$ in the high-spin bin. The Richardson-extrapolated field
has a median relative $L^2$ error of $0.008\%$, well below the hFNO
error. The hFNO approximation error therefore dominates the
$0.008\%$ Richardson-extrapolation error.

\paragraph{QNM accuracy.} Under retrospective Leaver-closest
reporting, the median hFNO frequency error remains below $0.53\%$ in
every spin bin. QNM errors are reported for the hFNO and
fine reference fields only. Across the $128$ configurations, the
retrospective Leaver-closest frequency errors are $0.33\%$ for the
hFNO and $0.05\%$ for the fine reference field. In the
high-spin bin, the corresponding errors are $0.38\%$ and $0.02\%$.
The median damping-time errors across all configurations are $1.52\%$
and $0.27\%$, respectively. The Leaver-closest estimator is selected
separately for each field and for frequency and damping time.
Appendix~\ref{app:kerr-extractors} reports every extractor without
retrospective selection of the best-performing method. The late-time residual in
Fig.~\ref{fig:kerr-ringdown} is consistent with the gridwise MSE
giving greater weight to the high-amplitude burst than to the
low-amplitude exponential tail.
